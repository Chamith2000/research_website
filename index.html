AI-Powered Personalized Mathematics Learning Platform for Elementary Students

MathsBuddy is an AI-powered personalized mathematics learning platform for Grade 3, 4, and 5 students. It integrates adaptive difficulty and dynamic MCQ generation, continuous progress monitoring with personalized recommendations, AI-powered content quality and feedback analysis, and privacy-preserving emotion-aware engagement with academic-integrity monitoring.

Adaptive Difficulty & MCQ Generation Recommends suitable difficulty levels and generates topic-based MCQs according to each learner's performance.
LSTM Progress Monitoring Analyzes learner history to forecast future performance trends and support early intervention.
Content Quality & Feedback Analysis Evaluates questions, teacher guides, and student feedback to identify content issues and improvement areas.
Emotion & Integrity Monitoring Detects engagement signals and academic integrity concerns during remote learning sessions.
Research Domain

Personalized Mathematics Learning for Elementary Students

The research designs a full-stack Grade 3–5 mathematics platform that connects diagnostic adaptive practice, LSTM-based forecasting, Qwen-based content analysis, and privacy-preserving browser-side engagement and integrity monitoring.

01

Literature Survey

Elementary mathematics learners progress at different speeds, but traditional worksheets and many LMS platforms provide the same activity to all students and rely mainly on correctness or final marks.

02

Research Gap

Reviewed systems rarely combine adaptive difficulty, dynamic question generation, long-term progress forecasting, content-quality analysis, and privacy-preserving engagement/integrity monitoring in one elementary mathematics platform.

03

Research Problem

The central problem is how a learning platform can transform diverse learner and content evidence into personalized, trustworthy, and teacher-actionable support without replacing educator judgment.

04

Objectives & Methodology

The methodology integrates four components: adaptive difficulty and dynamic MCQ generation, continuous progress monitoring and recommendation, AI-powered content quality and feedback analysis, and emotion-aware integrity monitoring.

05

Key Technologies & Results

React, Node.js/Express, Flask, MongoDB, Qwen2.5-Math with QLoRA, Qwen-based LoRA models, LSTM, ARIMA baseline, MobileNetV2, YOLOv8, and TensorFlow.js; reported results include 69.20% difficulty accuracy, 1.59 count MAE, LSTM RMSE 0.10, 70% topic matching, and 90% video-feedback accuracy.
